Wall painting, also known as mural painting, is a form of artistic expression that involves creating paintings directly on walls or large surfaces. This art form has a rich history dating back to ancient civilizations, where murals were used to depict stories, myths, religious beliefs, and cultural practices.

Music notation typically means the written expression of music notes and rhythms on paper using symbols. When music is written down, the pitches and rhythm of the music, such as the notes of a melody, are notated. Music notation often provides instructions on how to perform the music. For example, the sheet music for a song may state the song is a "slow blues" or a "fast swing", which indicates the tempo and the genre. To read notation, a person must have an understanding of music theory, harmony and the performance practice associated with a particular song or piece's genre. Written notation varies with the style and period of music. Nowadays, notated music is produced as sheet music or, for individuals with computer scorewriter programs, as an image on a computer screen. In ancient times, music notation was put onto stone or clay tablets.To perform music from notation, a singer or instrumentalist requires an understanding of the rhythmic and pitch elements embodied in the symbols and the performance practice that is associated with a piece of music or genre. In genres requiring musical improvisation, where only the chord changes and form of the song are written,requiring the performer to have a great understanding of the music's structure, harmony and the styles of a particular genre e.g., jazz or country music. In Western art music, the most common types of written notation are scores, which include all the music parts of an ensemble piece, and parts, which are the music notation for the individual performers or singers. In popular music, jazz, and blues, the standard musical notation is the lead sheet, which notates the melody, chords, lyrics, and structure of the music.

Mandala art is a mesmerizing form of expression that originates from ancient spiritual traditions, particularly in Hinduism and Buddhism. The word "mandala" itself means "circle" in Sanskrit, symbolizing the universe and the interconnectedness of all things. Mandala art is characterized by intricate geometric patterns and designs radiating from a central point, often representing unity, harmony, and balance. Artists create mandalas using various mediums such as paint, sand, or digital tools, each piece unique and reflective of the creator's inner world. Beyond its aesthetic beauty, mandala art is also used as a meditative practice, inviting contemplation and mindfulness through its repetitive and symmetrical motifs. n contemporary art, mandalas continue to inspire artists worldwide, blending traditional motifs with modern interpretations. The allure of mandala art lies not only in its visual complexity but also in its ability to evoke deep emotions, introspection, and a sense of interconnectedness with the cosmos. In conclusion, mandala art transcends mere decoration to become a profound expression of spirituality, culture, and artistic exploration. Its timeless appeal and universal symbolism make it a cherished art form that continues to captivate and inspire people across cultures and generations.

Shivaji Maharaj, also known as Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, was a visionary leader and legendary warrior who rose to prominence in 17th-century India. Born in 1630 in the Bhonsle family, Shivaji Maharaj inherited a small territory in the western part of India. Through his strategic acumen, military prowess, and strong leadership, he transformed this small principality into the formidable Maratha Empire, which became a symbol of resistance against foreign powers, particularly the Mughal Empire. Shivaji Maharaj's reign is characterized by his innovative military strategies, such as guerrilla warfare tactics and the effective use of forts, which allowed him to successfully challenge and expand his territory despite facing much larger and more powerful adversaries. His administration was known for its focus on justice, equality, and religious tolerance, earning him the respect and loyalty of people from diverse backgrounds. Beyond his military and administrative achievements, Shivaji Maharaj was a patron of arts, culture, and literature. He promoted Marathi language and literature, supported scholars and poets, and encouraged the development of architecture and craftsmanship. His legacy continues to inspire generations, with numerous monuments, forts, and institutions dedicated to preserving his memory and teachings.

Painting is a timeless form of artistic expression that has captivated and inspired people throughout history. It encompasses a wide range of techniques, styles, and mediums, each offering unique opportunities for creativity and self-expression. Two essential elements of painting that significantly impact the artistic process and final outcome are brushes and color. Brushes are the tools that artists use to apply paint to a surface, whether it's canvas, paper, wood, or any other material. They come in various shapes, sizes, and materials, each serving a specific purpose in creating different textures, lines, and effects. For example, flat brushes are ideal for broad strokes and filling large areas, while round brushes are suitable for detailed work and creating fine lines. The bristles of a brush can be made from natural materials like sable, hog hair, or synthetic fibers, each offering unique properties such as flexibility, absorbency, and durability.Color plays a pivotal role in painting, evoking emotions, conveying moods, and bringing life to artistic compositions. Artists have a vast palette of colors to choose from, including primary colors (red, blue, yellow), secondary colors (green, orange, purple), and tertiary colors (a mix of primary and secondary colors). Additionally, artists can manipulate colors through techniques such as blending, shading, tinting, and toning, creating endless possibilities for creating depth, dimension, and visual interest in their paintings. Furthermore, the application of paint using different brush techniques can greatly influence the texture and appearance of a painting. Techniques such as stippling, dry brushing, glazing, and impasto can create a variety of effects, from smooth and blended transitions to textured and expressive surfaces.

The Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV) is a family of expendable launch vehicles developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) to launch satellites into geostationary transfer orbits (GTO) and geostationary orbits (GEO). The GSLV consists of three main stages: the core first stage, a liquid-fueled Vikas engine stage, and a cryogenic upper stage powered by a CE-7.5 cryogenic engine. The cryogenic stage enables the GSLV to carry heavier payloads and achieve higher altitudes compared to earlier launch vehicles like the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV).The GSLV has been instrumental in launching several important missions, including communication satellites like INSAT and GSAT series, navigation satellites like IRNSS (NavIC), and scientific missions like Chandrayaan-2. Its success has strengthened India's position in the global space industry and demonstrated ISRO's expertise in launch vehicle technology. One of the notable features of the GSLV is its indigenous cryogenic upper stage, which India developed after overcoming technological challenges and mastering cryogenic propulsion technology. This achievement marked a significant milestone in India's space program, enhancing its capability to launch communication satellites, weather satellites, and other payloads into geostationary orbits.
